Electric Cars & Sedans
CCS2 / CHAdeMO · Type 2
The most common EV segment on Kerala roads. These vehicles support both AC home charging and DC fast charging at public stations. Battery sizes typically range from 30–82 kWh, giving a real-world range of 250–450 km per charge depending on driving conditions and AC usage.

Electric SUVs
CCS2 · DC Rapid 100–150 kW
Larger battery packs (60–100 kWh) mean longer range but also higher energy needs at charging stops. DC rapid chargers at 100–150 kW are ideal for highway charging, adding 100–150 km of range in just 15–20 minutes — critical for Kerala's expanding intercity EV corridors like NH 66 and NH 544.

Light Commercial EVs & Vans
Type 2 IEC 62196 · AC Fast 22 kW
Electric delivery vans and light commercial vehicles are rapidly expanding across Kerala's logistics sector — especially in Ernakulam, Thiruvananthapuram, and Kozhikode urban zones. These vehicles typically charge during loading/unloading windows at AC fast chargers, making Type 2 (22 kW) the primary standard for this category.

Electric Two-Wheelers
Bharat AC-001 · AC Slow ≤7 kW
The fastest-growing EV segment in Kerala, driven by high petrol prices and the emergence of premium electric scooters. Most two-wheelers charge via the Bharat AC-001 standard (a 15A domestic socket equivalent) and can fully charge overnight at home or in 3–4 hours at a dedicated slow charger. Battery swapping is also available on select models.

Electric Auto-Rickshaws
Bharat AC-001 · AC Slow / AC Fast
Electric auto-rickshaws have become a staple of urban mobility in Thiruvananthapuram, Ernakulam, and Kozhikode. They typically charge during driver breaks or overnight at slow chargers. Kerala's auto-rickshaw EV fleet is growing rapidly with government incentives supporting adoption, making dedicated slow and medium charging points in city centres essential infrastructure.

Electric Buses
CCS2 / CHAdeMO · DC Fast ≥50 kW
KSRTC Kerala has been steadily expanding its electric bus fleet on intra-city and short intercity routes. Electric buses carry 200–400 kWh battery packs and require high-power DC chargers at depots and key intercity hubs. Overnight depot charging is standard, but mid-route opportunity charging (30–45 min stops) is increasingly used on longer corridors.
