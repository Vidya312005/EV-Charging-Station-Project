🔍 What is VoltDrive?

VoltDrive is an intelligent EV charging station management and prediction platform built specifically for Kerala's fast-growing electric vehicle ecosystem. It combines live real-world data with algorithmic intelligence to help both EV drivers find the nearest available charger and infrastructure planners identify where new stations are urgently needed.

The platform fetches live data from the OpenChargeMap API — the world's largest open database of EV charging locations — and processes 8 key parameters per station: address, coordinates, number of charging points, connection type, power output, usage type, operator name, and operational status. This rich data feeds directly into VoltDrive's prediction and scheduling engine.

Real-time port availability is simulated every 15–45 seconds using time-based traffic models. Rush-hour windows (8–10 AM and 6–8 PM) trigger higher utilisation rates, while late-night hours reflect near-full availability — mirroring real EV driver behaviour patterns observed across Kerala.

🔮 The Prediction Engine

VoltDrive's Future Station Predictor uses a multi-factor greedy algorithm to evaluate all 14 Kerala districts and rank them by how urgently they need new charging infrastructure. Every score is computed entirely from live OCM data — nothing is hardcoded.

📡 Coverage Gap (22%)
Haversine distance to the nearest station. Districts beyond 30 km from any charger receive maximum urgency scores.
🏗️ Infrastructure Density (18%)
Total charging points relative to EV density. Zero stations = score of 100 (maximum need).
⚡ Power Profile (14%)
Identifies which speed tiers are missing — DC Fast, AC Fast, or AC Slow — and penalises gaps in the charging speed mix.
🟢 Operational Uptime (14%)
Ratio of live to total stations. Low uptime districts get a higher urgency score indicating reliability issues.
🌍 Public Access (12%)
Proportion of stations that are publicly accessible. Predominantly private-access districts score higher for public deployment need.
🔌 Connector Variety (12%)
Checks for missing standards: CCS2/CHAdeMO, Type 2 (IEC 62196), and Bharat AC-001. Missing standards leave vehicle categories underserved.
